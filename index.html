About RGS Academy

Sport as a Tool for Change

RGS Academy is a grassroots sports NGO founded by Sanjeev Choudhary (Sonu RGS) in Jujharpur, Narmadapuram, Madhya Pradesh. We believe that sport is one of the most powerful tools to transform the lives of children — building discipline, confidence, teamwork, and a hunger to succeed both on and off the field.


From dusty village grounds to organized tournaments, RGS Academy creates a new generation of youth who are physically fit, mentally strong, and empowered with education and opportunity. We work directly with children, families, schools, and local communities to make sport accessible to every child — regardless of background, gender, or economic status.

Our Approach

How We Work

Our model is simple but powerful — we meet children where they are, in their villages, and create a nurturing sporting environment that opens doors to bigger possibilities.

🔍
1

Identify & Reach Out

We identify underserved children in rural villages, work with local schools and parents to bring them into our program — especially girls who face the most barriers.

🏋️
2

Train & Develop

Professional sports coaching combined with academic support builds both physical ability and mental strength. Every child gets a kit, a coach, and a community.

🏆
3

Compete & Grow

Our children participate in local, district and state-level tournaments — giving them real-world exposure, confidence, and a pathway to a sporting career.

Meet The Founder

Sanjeev Choudhary

Sonu RGS — Founder, RGS Academy

Born and raised in Jujharpur, Sanjeev Choudhary (known as Sonu RGS) started RGS Academy with a simple belief: every child in rural India deserves access to sport, and sport can change lives. From organizing the first football sessions on a village ground to building one of Narmadapuram's most impactful youth programs, Sonu's journey is one of passion, grit, and deep love for his community.


His vision — that sport is the greatest equalizer — drives everything RGS Academy does. Under his leadership, the academy has grown from 30 children to 500+, and from one village to eight, with no signs of stopping.
